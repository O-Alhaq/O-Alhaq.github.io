Chocolate Chip Cookies

O_Alhaq Lv1
  2026-02-12 14:45 2026-02-12 14:45 Created   2026-02-12 15:03:07 2026-02-12 15:03:07 Updated  
  • Recipes 
 

Cookies are the basics of baking, but that doesn’t make them any less important. Here is my go-to recipe for cookies that are golden on the edges and soft in the center.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup (150g) brown sugar
  • ½ cup (113g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 206g all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Chocolate chips or chopped chocolate bars

Important Note: You might see gluten-free flour in the picture above—DO NOT USE IT! I tried it once as a request, and it simply does not work for this recipe. Gluten is what gives this cookie its necessary structure. Stick to standard All-Purpose flour.

Instructions

1. The Wet Ingredients

In a bowl (I will be using my infamous pink bowl), whisk together the granulated sugar, brown sugar, and melted butter until smooth.

  • Whisk in the whole egg, then add the egg yolk and whisk until fully incorporated.
  • Finally, whisk in the baking soda, vanilla extract, and salt.

2. The Dry Ingredients

Add the flour one half at a time. Fold the mixture with a spatula (or by hand) just until combined be careful not to overmix.

  • Add the chocolate chips/chunks and fold them into the dough.

3. Chill the Dough

Resting the dough allows the flour to hydrate and the flavors to deepen. You have two options here:

  • Option A : Cover the bowl and refrigerate for 3-4 hours (or overnight). Then, form the dough into equal-sized balls (I usually do ~52g portions).
  • Option B : Portion the dough into balls (~52g portions as well), then refrigerate them for about 1 hour. I prefer this method because I hate waiting.

4. Bake

Place the cookie dough balls on a parchment-lined baking tray with even spacing.

  • Temperature: Bake at ~185°C (approx. 365°F).
  • Time: 10-12 minutes.
  • Visual Check: Pull them out when the edges are golden brown but the centers still look slightly undone/soft.

5. Cool & Serve

Let them sit on the hot baking sheet for a couple of minutes to set before transferring them to a wire rack. Serve them warm while the chocolate is still melty, or enjoy them at room temperature.
