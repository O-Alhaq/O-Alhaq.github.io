ApplePie

What better way to use up an abundance of apples than to make a warm apple pie in winter! I will be sharing how I made my apple pie here, with many more recipes to come in the future.

Ingredients

Pie Crust

  • 3 cups flour (360g)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 220g COLD butter (cut into cubes)
  • ~½ cup ice water

Pie Filling

The Sauce:

  • 1 stick butter
  • 3 tablespoons flour
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • ¼ cup water (might need a splash more)
  • Juice of half a lemon (Optional)

The Apples:

  • 6-7 Granny Smith apples
  • Cinnamon to taste (I use about 1½ teaspoons)

Instructions

Part 1: The Pie Crust

Important Note: The butter and water NEED to be cold. Don’t make my mistakes! Before fully starting with the dough, I usually let my butter and water sit in the freezer for about 10 minutes to ensure they stay cold.

Credit: This recipe is adapted from Preppy Kitchen. You can watch this video to better understand the texture.

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: Add the salt, sugar, half of the flour, and the butter into a food processor. Run the processor for about 30 seconds until the mixture seems crumbly, like sand.
  2. Add Remaining Flour: Add the other half of the ingredients and pulse it a couple of times (about 2 to 3 times) to distribute the mixture.
  3. Add Water: Drizzle about 4 tablespoons of ice water and pulse it for a bit. What we are looking for is for the crumbly mixture to stick together when squeezed; it should hold its shape and not fall apart easily. Add a tablespoon of water at a time until you get the correct consistency.
  4. Chill: Transfer the dough onto a surface (preferably a silicone baking mat to minimize the mess). Form the crumbly dough into a ball—it might seem impossible, but trust the process! Apply pressure and it should hold its shape.
  5. Rest: Cut it into 2 pieces, wrap each one individually, and transfer the dough to a fridge for at least one hour (or leave it overnight).

Part 2: The Pie Filling

Note: The thickness of the apple slices determines the final texture. Thin slices result in a softer filling, while medium slices retain some crunch.

Reference: I used this video for the filling technique.

  1. Prep Apples: Peel and core the apples, then thinly slice them. Place them in a large bowl and sprinkle the cinnamon on top. I prefer to squeeze about half a lemon on top as it adds a little sour taste which enhances the apples (this is optional). Combine these ingredients.
  2. Make Sauce: In a medium-sized pan, melt the butter, then whisk in the flour and let it simmer for 1 minute. Whisk in the ¼ cup of water and ¾ cup of sugar and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat and continue whisking for 3 minutes while it simmers, then remove from the heat.
  3. Combine: Add the sauce to the apples then stir to coat the apple slices.

Part 3: Assembling the Pie

Note 1: When rolling the dough, keep it moving and lift it a bit to make sure that it isn’t stuck. Keep the surface floured to avoid sticking.
Note 2: If you think the dough is too cold, let it sit outside the fridge for a minute or two before rolling it out.

  1. Roll Base: Sprinkle your work surface with flour and roll out the dough according to your mold. I used a 24 cm metal mold which was a bit deep, so I rolled the dough to about 26 cm. Adjust the size according to what you have. Transfer the dough into the mold (you can lift it or roll it onto the rolling pin, which is the safest option in my experience).
  2. Fill: Add the filling to the mold and make sure it is evenly distributed.
  3. Lattice Top: Roll the second piece of dough into a circular shape and cut it into strips. Arrange the strips in a woven lattice pattern (or keep it simple!).
  4. Wash: Beat together 1 egg and 1 tbsp water (or milk). Brush the top with this mixture and sprinkle some sugar on top.
  5. Bake: Bake at ~200˚C for about 15 minutes, then reduce the heat to ~180˚C for 45 minutes.
    • My Experience: I used an electric oven and only had the heat on from above for the last 10 minutes to avoid the pie burning, as my oven tends to do that.
  6. Cool: Remove when the pie is golden in color and the filling is bubbling through the surface. Let it rest for about 1 hour.
